# NoteGenius AI — Feature Reference

Complete guide to all AI features with real-world scenario examples.

---

## Existing Features

### 1. Summarize
**What it does:** Condenses your entire note into clear bullet points.

**Scenario:**
> You just came back from a 2-hour product meeting and your note is 600 words long. You click **Summarize** and instantly get 6 bullet points covering decisions made, blockers, and next steps — perfect for sharing with your team.

---

### 2. Expand
**What it does:** Takes short bullet points or rough ideas and turns them into detailed paragraphs.

**Scenario:**
> You jotted down: "launch blog → weekly posts → SEO → hire writer". You click **Expand** and the AI writes a full content marketing strategy paragraph from those 6 words.

---

### 3. Fix Grammar
**What it does:** Corrects all grammar, spelling, and punctuation errors. Replaces your note content with the corrected version.

**Scenario:**
> You typed notes quickly during a lecture and made many typos. You click **Fix Grammar** and your note is instantly clean and readable, ready to share with classmates.

---

### 4. Key Points
**What it does:** Extracts the most important points and action items as a numbered list.

**Scenario:**
> After writing stream-of-consciousness notes during a client call, you click **Key Points** and get a numbered list of the 5 most important things the client said — including their deadline and budget constraint you almost missed.

---

### 5. ELI5 (Explain Like I'm 5)
**What it does:** Rewrites complex content in simple, everyday language.

**Scenario:**
> You pasted a dense paragraph about quantum computing from a research paper. You click **ELI5** and get a version that explains it using the analogy of a coin spinning — something you can actually understand.

---

### 6. Rewrite As
**What it does:** Rewrites your note in a different tone — Professional, Casual, Academic, Persuasive, or Creative — while keeping the meaning the same.

**Scenario:**
> You drafted a casual complaint about a software bug. Before sending it to your manager, you click **Rewrite As → Professional** and it becomes a clear, formal bug report with no emotional language.

---

### 7. Translate To
**What it does:** Translates your note to another language. Appends the translation below the original.

**Scenario:**
> You wrote meeting notes in English but your client only reads Spanish. You click **Translate To → Spanish** and the full translation is appended below your original, keeping both versions in one note.

---

## New Smart Tools

### 8. ✨ Generate Title
**What it does:** Reads your note content and suggests 3 clever, descriptive titles. Click any title to apply it instantly.

**Scenario:**
> You wrote 3 paragraphs about your new business idea but just called it "Untitled Note". You click **Generate Title** and get three options: "The Subscription Box Problem", "Why Convenience Beats Price Every Time", and "A Business Idea Born from Frustration". You click the first one and the title is set instantly.

---

### 9. ✅ Action Items
**What it does:** Scans your note for tasks, to-dos, and commitments and formats them as a markdown checklist.

**Scenario:**
> After a brainstorming session you have a messy note full of ideas mixed with tasks. You click **Action Items** and get a clean checklist:
> - [ ] Research competitor pricing
> - [ ] Schedule call with designer
> - [ ] Write landing page copy by Friday
>
> You can copy this directly into your project management tool.

---

### 10. 🎭 Analyze Tone
**What it does:** Analyzes the emotional tone and writing style of your note and gives one actionable improvement tip.

**Scenario:**
> You wrote a follow-up email draft in your notes. You click **Analyze Tone** and learn: "The tone is formal but slightly defensive in the third paragraph. The writing style is structured and clear. Suggestion: Replace 'I must point out' with 'I'd like to highlight' to soften the tone without losing authority."

---

### 11. # Hashtags
**What it does:** Generates 8-10 relevant hashtags for your note — useful for categorizing notes or repurposing content for social media.

**Scenario:**
> You wrote a note about your experience learning TypeScript. You click **Hashtags** and get:
> `#typescript #webdevelopment #coding #javascript #programming #learningnotes #frontend #devjourney #typescript2026`
>
> You paste these into your tweet when sharing the note publicly.

---

### 12. ➕ Continue Writing
**What it does:** Reads your note and writes the next natural paragraph that continues your thoughts in your exact writing style.

**Scenario:**
> You wrote two paragraphs of a blog post about remote work but hit writer's block. You click **Continue Writing** and the AI writes a third paragraph in your casual, first-person style that flows perfectly from where you stopped. You edit it slightly and keep writing.

## Power Analysis Tools (Unique to NoteGenius AI)

### 13. 🔍 Blind Spots
**What makes it unique:** No other note app audits your own writing for what you *forgot* to say. Every tool shows you what you wrote — this one shows you what you missed.

**What it does:** Acts as a critical reader and identifies 4-6 specific gaps, unanswered questions, and logical blind spots that a reader would notice in your note. Each item is labeled as "Missing:", "Unanswered:", or "Gap:" for clarity.

**Scenario:**
> You wrote a detailed plan for launching a SaaS product — pricing, features, target market. You feel confident. You click **Blind Spots** and the AI returns: "Unanswered: How will you handle customer support in the first 90 days? Gap: No mention of how you'll acquire your first 10 paying customers. Missing: What happens if the main competitor drops their price by 50%?" You realise your launch plan had three critical holes you hadn't thought about. You spend the next hour filling them in — and your plan is now investor-ready.

---

### 14. 🧠 First Principles
**What makes it unique:** No other note app strips your idea down to its atomic truths and rebuilds it from scratch the way Elon Musk or Aristotle would. Most AI tools summarise — this one deconstructs.

**What it does:** Applies first-principles thinking to the main idea in your note. Surfaces hidden assumptions, identifies the 3 most fundamental truths about the topic, then reconstructs the idea from those foundations in plain language.

**Scenario:**
> You have a note about why your startup needs to raise venture capital. You click **First Principles** and the AI reveals: "ASSUMPTIONS: That growth requires outside capital. That investors add strategic value. That the company cannot generate revenue fast enough to self-fund. FUNDAMENTALS: Capital accelerates the speed of hiring. Hiring increases execution capacity. Execution capacity determines how fast you reach product-market fit. REBUILT: You need outside capital only if you've confirmed product-market fit and the main bottleneck is headcount — not if you're still discovering what to build." You realise you were about to raise too early and pivot your fundraising strategy.

---

### 15. 😈 Devil's Advocate
**What makes it unique:** Most AI tools encourage you and help you polish your ideas. This one argues against you — hard. It steelmans the opposing view so you can stress-test your thinking before someone else does it for you in a meeting or pitch.

**What it does:** Reads your note and generates 4 sharp, specific counterarguments against your main idea or plan. Ends with a "Strongest objection" summary sentence that crystallises the most powerful challenge to your thinking.

**Scenario:**
> You wrote a note convincing yourself to quit your job and freelance full-time. You click **Devil's Advocate** and get: "1. You've never had to find clients with no employer brand backing you. 2. Your current lifestyle costs assume a salary — freelance income is irregular for the first 12 months. 3. Your network is almost entirely within your current company and will shrink when you leave. 4. You're confusing disliking your manager with disliking employment. Strongest objection: You have no paying clients lined up, which means you're betting your financial stability on a skill (sales) you've never tested." You pause, line up two clients first, and then quit from a position of strength.

---

### 16. ⏳ Time Capsule
**What makes it unique:** No other app lets you hear from your future self. This isn't a summary or a rewrite — it's a perspective shift. It gives you emotional distance from your current situation by simulating the wisdom you'll have 5 years from now.

**What it does:** Rewrites your entire note as a message from your future self, 5 years in the future, looking back at this exact moment. The result is warm, specific, and honest — not generic motivational fluff. Because it replaces the note, it creates a time capsule artifact you can re-read later.

**Scenario:**
> You wrote a note during a really difficult week — you're anxious about a big presentation, worried about your career direction, and second-guessing a major decision. You click **Time Capsule** and the note becomes: "Looking back, that presentation felt like everything was riding on it, but the truth is it was just one moment in a much longer story. The decision you were agonising over — whether to take the product role or stay in engineering — turned out to be less important than how you showed up every day after making it. The anxiety you felt that week was real, but it was also a sign that you cared. And caring, it turned out, was the thing that made all the difference." You save it. Two years later you re-read it and it gives you exactly the perspective you needed in that moment.

---

### 17. 🎯 Stress Test
**What makes it unique:** No other note app tries to break your plan before reality does. Other tools summarise, expand, or polish. This one hunts for the single fragile assumption that could cause everything to collapse — and tells you what to watch for.

**What it does:** Subjects the plan, idea, or decision in your note to a structured stress test. Identifies the weakest point, the worst realistic failure scenario, the first early warning sign that things are going wrong, and the one survival question you must answer before proceeding.

**Scenario:**
> You have a note outlining a plan to launch an online course — content outline, pricing, launch date, marketing channel (Instagram). You click **Stress Test** and get: "WEAKEST POINT: The entire plan assumes you already have an engaged Instagram audience, but your account has 340 followers. If that assumption is wrong, your launch has no distribution. WORST CASE: You spend 3 months creating the course, launch to your 340 followers, sell 2 copies, and lose motivation entirely. EARLY WARNING: If your next 3 Instagram posts get fewer than 50 organic interactions each, your distribution is not ready. SURVIVAL QUESTION: Do you have at least 1,000 engaged followers or an email list before your launch date?" You realise you need to spend 2 months building the audience first, and you rewrite your timeline before writing a single lesson.

## Tips for Power Users

- **Chain actions:** Use **Expand** to flesh out bullet points, then **Fix Grammar** to clean it up, then **Rewrite As → Professional** to polish it — all in under 30 seconds.
- **Use Key Points before meetings:** Write messy notes, then extract the action items with **Action Items** extractor.
- **Title last:** Write your full note first, then use **Generate Title** for the perfect name.
- **Use Continue Writing to beat writer's block:** Even if the AI paragraph isn't perfect, it gets you unstuck.
- **Tone check before sending:** Always run **Analyze Tone** on anything you're sending to a client or manager.
